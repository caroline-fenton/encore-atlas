## Problem Statement

Most music platforms are optimized for polished, studio-recorded content and passive listening. They fail to capture the energy, imperfection, and cultural context of live performance.

Fans who want to relive concerts or explore live music moments face a fragmented experience. Content is scattered across platforms like YouTube, which prioritizes broad engagement over deep exploration of a single artist, era, or scene.

There is no cohesive product that supports the emotional, immersive experience of live music consumption—where users can follow a thread of energy, era, or scene rather than simply play isolated tracks.
