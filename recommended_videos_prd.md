**Recommended Videos**

---

**Why this exists**

Encore Atlas originally relied on search to drive engagement. After watching a video, users had no clear next step unless they manually searched again.

This created a fragile experience where users dropped off despite wanting to continue exploring.

Live music consumption is associative and emotional. Fans don’t just want one video — they want more of the same energy, era, or feeling.

---

**What this does**

After a user watches a video, Encore Atlas surfaces recommended videos that are contextually related.

Recommendations are based on:
- similar artists
- shared genres or eras
- inferred “vibe”
- what other users have searched for or watched

Users can continue watching without returning to search, creating a continuous experience.

---

**Where it shows up**

- Below the “More Live Sets” grid as **“You Might Also Like”**
- In a dedicated **“For You” tab** alongside Live Shows, Music Videos, Interviews, and Merch

This allows both passive discovery and intentional exploration.

---

**Experience shift**

Before:
Search → Watch → Stop

After:
Search → Watch → Discover → Continue → Explore

---

**Constraints & tradeoffs**

- Only 4 videos shown due to API limits
- Recommendation quality is initially limited
- “What others searched” may not always feel relevant
- Results improve as more users generate data

---

**Success looks like**

- 2%+ of users click a recommended video
- Users stay longer when engaging with recommendations
- Users explore more artists per session

---

**What’s next**

- Personalized recommendations based on user history
- User profiles with shareable recommendations
- Commentary and interaction around videos
